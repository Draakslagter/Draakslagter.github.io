About Me


Hi, I’m Simon — a passionate and imaginative game design student based in Cape Town, South Africa. I believe that stories are the heart of great games — not just as plot devices, but as tools for connection, emotion, and meaning. Whether it's branching dialogue in Visual Novel or character arcs in a Indie RPG, I'm driven to create experiences that resonate.

I'm currently studying toward my BA in Game Design at SAE Institute, where I’m honing my technical game-design skills. My academic journey has exposed me to a wide range of disciplines, from scripting, to systems thinking and collaborative project development. I'm particularly drawn to narrative systems, world-building, and player choice as design tools.

In terms of tools, I'm proficient in C#, Unity, JavaScript, CSS, and Twine, and I'm comfortable adapting to new engines and systems. I love exploring the space where narrative and mechanics meet — designing dialogue systems, integrating lore into gameplay, and shaping interactive stories that players care about.

Beyond the classroom, I'm a lifelong gamer and storyteller. I regularly run Dungeons & Dragons campaigns, which has sharpened my improvisation, pacing, and collaborative storytelling skills. I also play Magic: The Gathering, which constantly teaches me about game balance, mechanics, and strategic design. And of course, I play a wide range of video games, both for enjoyment and inspiration.

I'm always on the lookout for creative collaborations, internships, and opportunities to grow as a designer. If you’re working on a project that needs a narrative voice or design support — or if you just want to chat about games and storytelling — I’d love to hear from you.
